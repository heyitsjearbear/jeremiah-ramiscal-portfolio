My Story

Aspiring software engineer, aspiring data scientist, aspiring web developer... blah blah blah. To be honest it's all cookie cutter. I'll go further than that.

Growing Up

Growing up in an Asian-American household, I went through the motions, satisying the expectations that my parents set for me. I of course didn't want to dissapoint them, as they had sacrificed so much for my siblings and I to live a comfortable life. In high school, I immersed myself in my studies and extra-curriculars to give myself that edge when it came to college applications. That was fine and all, but in the blink of an eye, everything moved so fast. It was almost like I was trying to grow up too fast.

In the present

To preface what I am about to say, I do not condone succumbing to a "victim's mentality", though we all "feel" our emotions differently. As of writing this I am pursing my bachelors in Computer Science. So far in my college career, it seems like I have pushed important people in my life away, for the reason of wanting to be successful. To the half of you reading, you're probably thinking, "What's so wrong with that? You just want to be successful." It's true, I do want to be successful. So far though in my college career I've picked school and work over maintaining relationships with the friends and people I love.

For the Future

I hope to learn how to balance work and school with relationships. Human interaction in my opinion is something essential to living the fullest life. I am not perfect, I'll give myself that. But, there are things to work on. As of writing this, I must learn how to make experiences for myself, without having to depend on anyone to make them with/for me. I am a work-in progress. Life is so much more than working toward the inevitable 9-5 I desire.

"The good life is a process, not a state of being. It is a direction not a destination."

- Carl Rogers
